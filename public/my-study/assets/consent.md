## Purpose of the Study

The purpose of this study is to examine how people emotionally respond to different visualizations depicting the risks associated with chronic kidney disease, such as kidney failure and mortality. We want to find out which types of visuals are easiest to read and most helpful for communicating kidney failure and mortality risk, and how people feel when presented with this information.

## What Will I Be Asked to Do?

You will be asked to:

1. Complete a short demographic questionnaire covering age, gender and education.
2. Complete a survey to assess visual literacy. 
3. Review a brief scenario and several visualizations presenting risk information for a hypothetical person with chronic kidney disease and answer a short online survey (about 20–30 minutes) about your emotional responses, experience, and preferences for each visualization.

Your responses will be stored without your name or email attached.

## What Type of Personal Information Will Be Collected?

If you agree to participate, you will be asked to provide:
- Basic demographic information such as:
    - Age,
    - Gender,
    - Highest level of education.

No social insurance numbers, ID numbers, or medical record numbers will be collected.

This study does not require audio or video recordings. Your responses to the questionnaire will be stored in a password-protected university-hosted data storage space.

## Are there Risks or Benefits if I Participate?

This is a minimal risk study. The main inconvenience is the time it takes to complete the study (about 20–30 minutes). You may experience mild screen fatigue or brief frustration if a chart is difficult to interpret. You may skip any questions you do not wish to answer, and you may stop at any time. No sensitive or personal questions about trauma, mental health, or illegal activities will be asked.

There are no direct personal benefits to you. You may find it interesting to see different ways of displaying kidney failure and mortality risks. The results of the study may help improve how chronic kidney disease risk information is presented to patients and clinicians and may support clearer communication and shared decision-making.

After we confirm the validity of your responses, you will receive $8 CAD through prolific. If you do not complete the study, no payment will be issued. There are no costs for you to take part in the study.

## What Happens to the Information I Provide?

You are free to withdraw from this study at any point before submitting your survey by closing the survey window. The online survey is only recorded when it is submitted; therefore, there will be no partial survey data for participants who close the survey before submitting it. Once you submit the survey, withdrawal of your data may not be possible because the survey data will be de-identified and will not contain direct identifying information.
 
All data received from this study will be kept for 5 years in a secure location. Only the investigators indicated on this form will have access to this raw data.
